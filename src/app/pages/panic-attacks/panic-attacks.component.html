What is a Panic Attack?

A panic attack is a sudden wave of fear and anxiety, often without a real threat, accompanied by physical symptoms like sweating and a racing heartbeat, lasting anywhere from several minutes to an hour.

They can occur unexpectedly or be triggered by external stressors. Panic disorder is a common mental health condition estimated to affect nearly 5% of the US population. The good news: panic attacks and panic disorder are highly treatable.

With proper treatment and support, you can learn to manage panic attacks and regain control over your life.
